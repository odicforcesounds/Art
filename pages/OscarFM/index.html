Life and Death

Sun Wukong is a simple monkey who spend his life dreaming and writing about the dreams he had. This monkey is not a king, as society did not consider him as such, but he knew he could be a king on his own. That's how he continues to live, but this monkey always has something to say, and therefore, not all the animals in the jungle pay attention to the lessons he tries to teach. The animals in the jungle are subject to the condition of all living beings, conscious or not of their actions. This condition is called solipsism, which is a theory that is both true and false at the same time. This monkey is a creation of the author of this text, who also considers himself a bit of a monkey because the Chinese zodiac signs say that the author is a monkey. It is from this fantasy that the fire of this story is born and endures over time, and the author has decided to self-declare himself as "Sun Wukong". In fact the story of Sun Wukong is considered "the best story in Chinese culture," but the author of this text and this web page is far from China and has never had contact with it. The author is, from this moment on, Sun Wukong. I, Sun Wukong, would like to present a solution to the animal kingdom so that there are better choices and each animal is valued for their choices. Especially the predators, such as Tigers and Lions, Wolves, Bears, Dogs, and Cats. They should improve their social qualities and control their instinctive animality. Because they demonstrate more strength over other animals, they are the first to violate all the principles of their function in the jungle. In society and today, these animals have been associated with law enforcement, and therefore, they are seen as judicial members. Fortunately, the world has become less beastly than ancient history demonstrates, but the behavioral tendencies of these animals are ingrained in their souls, and sometimes, there are uncontrollable actions, as well as demonstrations of the development of immoral practices against the innocent. The Serpent should also know how to control its animality, as it is used by Medicine, an organization that kills thousands of animals in the pursuit of knowledge and the various solutions we know today. With that said, we should consider the purely military side, represented for centuries as the Dragon. The Dragon has compulsive problems with perfection and feels very uncomfortable when something is imperfect, and this "something" is any behavior. The Dragon accepts imperfection when it represents genuineness and naturalness, as well as honesty. The Dragon is a lover of the possibilities that animals can develop through their unbreakable and meticulously defined disciplines, based on the knowledge of centuries of the history of this planet. The oldest knowledge says that the Dragon will honor the Monkey King's choice. The monkey is not carnivorous, not a warrior who uses conventional weapons, but literary and musical weapons. This text is part of the message that the Dragon will carefully read in order to understand the reasons that motivated the Monkey King to make this decision, as well as to try to manipulate reality so that the monkey's dream is fulfilled. The predators feel victimized because they cannot use violent techniques to achieve their goals. The Monkey King knows that they are all capable of achieving the goal without the use of additional tools, beyond their social abilities. Courage must be associated with their ability to adapt to irregular circumstances and act in a subtle, polite, and precise manner. The capacity for self-control is taught to rational animals, but the education of irrational animals has the same effect. It doesn't matter whether the being is rational or not, but rather the educational process that should avoid, as much as possible, violent practices aimed at educating. Animals are not machines, and machines do not deserve to be violated because they have failed. The Monkey King has a dream that will create a positive revolution for the jungle world."
